<p><p>eHealth software and services refer to the use of information and communication technologies (ICT) to support and improve healthcare delivery. This includes electronic health records (EHR), telehealth services, mobile health applications, remote patient monitoring, and healthcare analytics, among others. The increasing adoption of digital technologies in the healthcare industry, the growing burden of chronic diseases, and the need to reduce healthcare costs and improve patient outcomes are some of the key drivers. The COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the adoption of eHealth solutions due to the need for remote healthcare delivery and minimizing in-person consultations.</p><p>The future outlook for the eHealth software and service market appears positive. These include concerns around data privacy and security, interoperability of different eHealth systems, ensuring access for underserved populations, and resistance to change from healthcare providers.
