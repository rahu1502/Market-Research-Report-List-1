<p><p>Hydraulic mulch additives can be categorized into two types: acidic and alkaline. Acidic additives are those with a low pH level, typically below 7, and are used to enhance the stability and binding properties of the mulch. They are known to increase the erosion control capabilities and help with seed germination. On the other hand, alkaline additives have a high pH level, generally above 7. They are primarily used to neutralize the soil acidity, improve its structure, and enhance nutrient availability for plants.</p></p>
