<p><p>High temperature cameras are devices used to capture thermal imagery in environments with extreme heat conditions. There are two types of sensors used in these cameras: CCD Sensor and CMOS Sensor. CCD Sensor stands for Charge-Coupled Device and is known for its excellent image quality and sensitivity. It is commonly used in high-end applications where accuracy and precision are crucial. On the other hand, CMOS Sensor, or Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor, offers lower power consumption, compact size, and faster image processing. It is widely used in consumer-grade applications due to its cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
