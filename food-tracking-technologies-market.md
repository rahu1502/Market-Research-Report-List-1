<p><p>Food tracking technologies encompass various types of systems used to track and monitor the movement and quality of food products. These technologies include RFID/RTLS, GPS, barcode, infrared, and biometrics. RFID/RTLS uses radio frequency identification or real-time location systems to track and manage food items. GPS technology allows for real-time tracking of food shipments throughout the supply chain. Barcodes provide unique identifiers for each food item, facilitating efficient inventory management and tracking. Infrared technology is used to monitor the temperature and quality of stored perishable food. Biometric systems utilize unique biological characteristics, such as fingerprints or iris scans, for secure access and authentication in food tracking applications.</p></p>
