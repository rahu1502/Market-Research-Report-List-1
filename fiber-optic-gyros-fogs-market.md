<p><p>Fiber Optic Gyros (FOGs) are sensors used for measuring rotational motion. Interferometric FOG utilizes the interference of light waves in a fiber to detect rotation and is widely used in aerospace and navigation applications. Resonant FOG employs resonating structures to sense rotation and is commonly used in unmanned vehicles and robotics. Stimulated Brillouin Scattering FOG utilizes the scattering of light waves by sound waves to detect rotation and is suitable for high-precision navigation and optical fiber communication systems. These FOG market types cater to different industries and applications.</p></p>
