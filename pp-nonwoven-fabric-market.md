<p><p>PP nonwoven fabric market comprises different types such as spunbonded, staples, meltblown, and composite materials. Spunbonded fabrics are produced by extruding fine filaments and bonding them together, offering high strength and stability. Staples are made by weaving or bonding short fibers, providing good durability and bulkiness. Meltblown fabrics involve the creation of fine fibers through a high-velocity air stream, offering excellent filtration properties. Composite materials combine multiple layers or types of PP fabric to provide enhanced performance and functionality. Each type caters to specific applications, such as packaging, hygiene products, automotive, and construction.</p></p>
