<p><p>The coconut coir product market can be classified into several types. Coir fibre refers to the fibrous material extracted from the husk of coconuts, which is widely used in the production of mattresses, upholstery, and erosion control products. Coir pith, also known as coir dust, is the spongy material left after the extraction of coir fibre. It is primarily used as a growing medium in horticulture and as a component in soil amendments. Coir yarn is produced by spinning coir fibre and is used in the manufacturing of various products like mats, rugs, and ropes. Curled coir, also called coir rope, is made by twisting coir fibre strands together and is commonly used in making mats and brushes. Additionally, the coconut coir product market includes various other products derived from coir, such as coir logs for erosion control, coir twine for agricultural purposes, and coir netting for slope stabilization.</p></p>
