<p><p>The Rheological Modifier market can be categorized into two types: Organic Rheological Modifiers and Inorganic Rheological Modifiers. Organic Rheological Modifiers refer to substances derived from natural sources, such as plant extracts or polymers, that enhance the flow properties and stability of various products. On the other hand, Inorganic Rheological Modifiers are compounds composed of minerals or metal ions that provide similar flow control and stability, but through different chemical mechanisms. Both types of modifiers are widely used in industries like paints, adhesives, and personal care products for their rheological properties.</p></p>
