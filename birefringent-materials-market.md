<p><p>Birefringent materials refer to substances that exhibit two different indices of refraction for light traveling through them. Uniaxial crystals are a type of birefringent material that has one preferred direction of optical propagation, resulting in two different refractive indices perpendicular to each other. On the other hand, biaxial crystals have two preferred directions of optical propagation, leading to three different refractive indices. This distinction between uniaxial and biaxial crystals enables specific applications in optics, such as polarization filters and waveplates, where light can be manipulated based on its polarization state.</p></p>
