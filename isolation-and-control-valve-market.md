<p><p>An isolation valve is a type of valve that is used to completely shut off the flow of a fluid or gas in a pipeline. It is typically installed to control the flow of media in a system, allowing for maintenance, repairs, or replacement of components without disrupting the entire system. On the other hand, a control valve is used to regulate the flow or pressure of a fluid within a system, maintaining and controlling the desired set point.</p><p>The isolation and control valve market is experiencing substantial growth due to the increasing demand for these valves in various industries such as oil and gas, power generation, water and wastewater treatment, chemical, and pharmaceutical. Industries are increasingly opting for valves that can withstand harsh operating conditions, such as high pressure, extreme temperatures, and corrosive environments. <p><p>The isolation and control valve market encompasses various types of valves, including ball valves, gate valves, globe valves, check valves, butterfly valves, plug valves, and others. Ball valves provide tight shut-off and have low pressure drops. Gate valves are primarily used for on/off applications and have a sliding gate mechanism. Globe valves offer precise flow control and have good shut-off capabilities. Check valves allow one-way flow and prevent backflow. Butterfly valves are quick-opening valves suitable for large-scale applications. Plug valves use a cylindrical or tapered plug to control flow. The "others" category includes different valve types that serve specific purposes in isolation and control applications.</p></p>
