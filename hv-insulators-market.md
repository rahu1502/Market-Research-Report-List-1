<p><p>HV Insulators are utilized in high-voltage power transmission systems to prevent the flow of electric current between the energized conductors and the supporting structures. There are three main types of HV Insulators in the market: Porcelain insulators, which are made of porcelain material and offer high mechanical strength; Glass insulators, which are manufactured from toughened glass and provide excellent resistance to weather conditions; and Composite insulators, which are made of a combination of materials like fiberglass and silicone rubber, offering lightweight design and increased resistance to pollution. These insulators cater to diverse requirements in various power transmission applications.</p></p>
