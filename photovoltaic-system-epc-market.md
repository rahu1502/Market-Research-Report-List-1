<p><p>The Photovoltaic System EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) market can be classified into two types: Centralized Photovoltaic EPC and Distributed Photovoltaic EPC. </p><p>Centralized Photovoltaic EPC refers to large-scale solar power projects where photovoltaic panels are installed in a centralized location and connected to the grid. These projects are typically managed by a single entity and require significant investment in infrastructure.</p><p>Distributed Photovoltaic EPC, on the other hand, involves smaller-scale solar installations, often on residential or commercial rooftops. These systems generate electricity onsite and are connected to the local grid, allowing users to consume the energy they produce and sell excess power back to the grid. Distributed Photovoltaic EPC projects are typically implemented by multiple entities or individual property owners.</p></p>
