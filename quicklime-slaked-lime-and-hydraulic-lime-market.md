<p><p>Quicklime, slaked lime, and hydraulic lime are all types of lime used in various applications. Quicklime, also known as calcium oxide (CaO), is a white crystalline powder obtained by heating limestone or seashells at high temperatures. It is primarily used in construction materials, such as cement, mortar, and plaster, due to its binding properties.</p><p>Slaked lime, or calcium hydroxide (Ca(OH)2), is produced by adding water to quicklime, resulting in a white powder or slurry. It is commonly used in agriculture to neutralize acidic soil and as a flocculating agent in water and wastewater treatment.</p><p>Hydraulic lime is derived from limestone containing clay minerals. It has the ability to set and harden underwater due to its hydraulic properties. It is used in building restoration, conservation, and natural construction applications.</p><p>The Quicklime, Slaked Lime, and Hydraulic Lime Market is expected to experience significant growth in the forecasted period.
