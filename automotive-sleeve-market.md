<p><p>The automotive sleeve market consists of two main types: dry-type sleeve and wet-type sleeve. In the dry-type sleeve market, the sleeves are designed to be self-lubricating and do not require additional lubrication. These sleeves are typically made from materials such as bronze or composite materials. On the other hand, wet-type sleeve market includes sleeves that require external lubrication to reduce friction and wear. These sleeves are often made from materials like cast iron and are commonly used in heavy-duty applications to ensure proper lubrication and enhance engine performance.</p></p>
