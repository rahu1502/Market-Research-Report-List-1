<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Battery grade graphite is divided into three types: crystalline flake graphite, amorphous graphite, and vein graphite (lump). Crystalline flake graphite is a natural graphite that has a flake-like structure, making it suitable for lithium-ion batteries due to its high conductivity. Amorphous graphite, on the other hand, has a non-crystalline structure and is primarily used in alkaline batteries. Vein graphite, also known as lump graphite, is a rare form of graphite that occurs as veins in crystalline rocks and is often used in specialty batteries for its high purity and unique properties.</p></p>
