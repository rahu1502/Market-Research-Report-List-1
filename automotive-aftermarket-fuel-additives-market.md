<p><p>Automotive aftermarket fuel additives can be classified into two types: gasoline additives and diesel additives. Gasoline additives are specifically formulated to enhance the performance of gasoline-powered vehicles. They aim to improve fuel efficiency, protect the engine from corrosion, reduce harmful emissions, and clean fuel injectors. On the other hand, diesel additives are designed to optimize the performance of diesel engines by improving their combustion efficiency, reducing carbon deposits, preventing fuel oxidation, preventing injector fouling, and providing lubrication to the fuel system components. Both types of additives are used to enhance the overall performance and longevity of the vehicle.</p></p>
