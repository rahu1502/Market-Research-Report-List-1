<p><p>The beverage stabilizers market consists of various types of stabilizers, including xanthan gum, carboxymethyl cellulose, carrageenan, and gum arabic. Xanthan gum is a polysaccharide that enhances viscosity, stability, and texture in beverages. Carboxymethyl cellulose is a cellulose derivative used as a thickening agent and stabilizer. Carrageenan is derived from seaweed and is utilized for its gelling and stabilizing properties. Gum arabic is a natural gum obtained from the Acacia Senegal tree and acts as an emulsifying and stabilizing agent in beverages. These stabilizers help maintain desired consistency, improve mouthfeel, and prevent sedimentation in various beverages.</p></p>
