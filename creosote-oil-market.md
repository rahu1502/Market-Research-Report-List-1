<p><p>Creosote oil is a type of tar oil that is derived from wood, primarily from the distillation of coal tar or from pyrolysis of plant-based materials. It is characterized by its dark color, strong smoky odor, and high carbon content. Creosote oil is commonly used as a wood preservative due to its excellent antifungal and insecticidal properties. It helps protect wood from decay, rot, and insect infestation, making it a popular choice for treating utility poles, railroad ties, fencing, and other outdoor structures.</p><p>The creosote oil market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
