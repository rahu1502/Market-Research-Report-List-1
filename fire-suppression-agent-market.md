<p><p>Fire suppression agents are substances used to control and extinguish fires. Water is the most common type and is used in various firefighting scenarios due to its effectiveness in cooling and smothering flames. Clean agents, such as halogenated and inert gases, are used in sensitive areas as they do not leave residue or cause damage. Foam fire suppression agents create a blanket over the fire, preventing oxygen from fueling it. Powder agents, like dry chemicals, work by forming a chemical reaction with flames, extinguishing the fire by interrupting the combustion process.</p></p>
