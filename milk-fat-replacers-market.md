Carbohydrate-Based Milk Fat Replacers include ingredients like maltodextrin or starch, offering emulsification and stabilization properties. Lipid-Based Milk Fat Replacers are usually made from vegetable oils and help enhance the flavor, mouthfeel, and creaminess of products. Each type of replacer serves the same purpose of mimicking the characteristics of milk fat in food products.</p></p>
