<p><p>The High-nickel Ternary Precursor market comprises three main types: NCM622, NCM811, and NCA. NCM622 refers to a lithium-ion battery precursor with a nickel-cobalt-manganese ratio of 6:2:2, while NCM811 has a nickel-cobalt-manganese ratio of 8:1:1. These precursors offer higher energy density and longer lifespan compared to their predecessors. NCA stands for lithium-nickel-cobalt-aluminum oxide and provides even higher energy density but at the expense of a shorter lifespan. These ternary precursors are crucial components in the manufacturing of advanced lithium-ion batteries for various applications like electric vehicles and energy storage systems.</p></p>
