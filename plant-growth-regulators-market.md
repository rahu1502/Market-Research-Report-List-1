<p><p>Plant growth regulators, also known as plant hormones, are substances that control various aspects of plant growth and development. There are three main types: auxins, cytokinins, and gibberellins. Auxins stimulate cell elongation, promote root growth, and control plant bending and tropisms. Cytokinins assist in cell division and differentiation, delaying tissue aging and stimulating shoot growth. Gibberellins regulate seed germination, stem elongation, and flowering induction. The market for these growth regulators is driven by the increasing demand for high-quality agricultural products and the need for improved crop yield, resulting in higher profitability for farmers.</p></p>
