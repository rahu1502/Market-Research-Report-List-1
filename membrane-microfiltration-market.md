<p><p>Membrane Microfiltration is a filtration process used to separate solids from liquids by passing a solution through a porous membrane. The market types for Membrane Microfiltration are divided into two categories: organic and inorganic. Organic membranes are made of natural polymers like cellulose and are generally used in applications where biological molecules need to be separated. In contrast, inorganic membranes are made of materials like ceramics or metals and are used in harsher chemical environments or to separate non-biological molecules. Both types offer different advantages and are chosen based on specific filtration requirements in various industries.</p></p>
