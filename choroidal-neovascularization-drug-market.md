<p><p>Choroidal neovascularization (CNV) drugs are medications used to treat a condition called choroidal neovascularization, which is characterized by the abnormal growth of blood vessels in the choroid layer of the eye. CNV can lead to vision impairment and even blindness if left untreated. Commonly prescribed CNV drugs include anti-VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor) medications, such as aflibercept, ranibizumab, and bevacizumab, which work by inhibiting the growth of abnormal blood vessels.</p><p>The current outlook of the CNV drug market is promising, with steady growth expected in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of age-related macular degeneration (AMD), one of the primary causes of CNV, is a significant driver for the demand of CNV drugs. The aging population, especially in developed countries, is expected to contribute to the growth of the CNV drug market.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in the healthcare sector, including improved diagnosis and treatment options, are expected to boost market growth. Emerging therapies, such as gene therapy and combination therapies, offer potential opportunities for market expansion. Additionally, the development of new drugs and improved drug delivery systems, such as sustained-release implants, may further enhance the market growth.</p><p>However, challenges such as high treatment costs and potential side effects of CNV drugs may limit market growth to some extent.
