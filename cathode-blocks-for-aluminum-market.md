<p><p>Cathode blocks for the aluminum market are categorized into three types: semi-graphitic, graphitic, and graphitized. Semi-graphitic blocks are composed of a mixture of graphite and semi-graphitic carbon, offering a balance between thermal conductivity and electrical resistivity properties. Graphitic blocks have a higher graphite content, providing improved electrical conductivity. Graphitized blocks have undergone additional heat treatment, resulting in a highly pure graphite structure with exceptional conductivity. These different market types enable aluminum producers to choose cathode blocks that best suit their specific requirements for efficient and reliable aluminum production.</p></p>
