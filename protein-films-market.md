<p><p>Protein films are thin, edible films made from proteins, which can be derived from various sources such as wheat, soy, whey, and gelatin. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for sustainable and eco-friendly packaging solutions has driven the adoption of protein films as an alternative to traditional plastic packaging. Protein films provide better protection against oxygen and moisture, ensuring longer shelf-life for food products. Secondly, the rising consumer awareness regarding the detrimental effects of plastic waste on the environment has fueled the demand for biodegradable packaging materials, further propelling the growth of the protein films market.</p><p>Furthermore, the food industry's focus on reducing food waste has also contributed to the market growth. Protein films can act as a barrier against microbial contamination, preserving food freshness and extending its shelf-life. <p><p>Protein films are a type of food packaging material made from various sources such as collagen, corn zein, soy protein, mung bean protein, gelatin, wheat gluten, and casein. Collagen films are derived from animal sources and are known for their strength and flexibility. Corn zein films are made from corn protein and offer good oxygen barrier properties. Soy protein films are plant-based and are biodegradable. Mung bean protein films have good mechanical properties and excellent water resistance. Gelatin films are derived from animal collagen and have good film-forming properties. Wheat gluten films are gluten-based and provide good oxygen barrier properties. Casein films are derived from milk and have excellent moisture barrier properties.</p></p>
