There are several types of organic photochromic materials available in the market. Naphthopyrans, Diarylethenes, Azobenzenes, Spiropyrans, and Hexaarylbiimidazole are some examples of these materials. Naphthopyrans are known for their thermal stability and reversible photochromism. Diarylethenes are highly efficient and can be easily switched between different colors. Azobenzenes exhibit photoisomerization, allowing them to change their shape and color. Spiropyrans are capable of reversible switching between two forms with different colors. Hexaarylbiimidazole materials are suitable for high-resolution holography applications.</p></p>
